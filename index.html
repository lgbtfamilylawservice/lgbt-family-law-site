When You Need an LGBTQ Family Law Attorney

Family law touches some of the most personal moments of your life — and for LGBTQ people, the legal terrain is rarely the same as it is for straight, cisgender families. A general family lawyer may know the statutes, but knowing the statutes is not the same as understanding what it means to be a non-biological mother fighting for custody, a trans man updating every legal document he owns, or a couple whose marriage was only legal in some states for some of the years it existed. That is why working with an experienced LGBTQ family law attorney matters.

Most people who call us are in one of these situations:

You are facing a same-sex divorce or domestic partnership dissolution.
You are a non-biological parent worried about custody after a breakup.
You and your partner are planning a pregnancy through surrogacy, IVF, or a known donor.
You need a second-parent or confirmatory adoption to protect your parentage.
You want to update your legal name or gender marker on your ID, birth certificate, or other documents.
You are getting married and want an LGBTQ-friendly prenup or postnup.
You need an estate plan that protects your spouse, partner, or chosen family.
You are in an emergency — a custody crisis, a domestic violence situation, or an urgent restraining order.

Whatever you are facing, calling (855) 218-9853 puts you in touch with someone who will listen first, then help you find the right LGBTQ family law attorney for your situation.

Why an LGBTQ-Affirming Family Law Attorney Matters

The wrong lawyer can quietly cost you a great deal — sometimes your parental rights, sometimes your assets, sometimes just years of feeling unseen by the person who is supposed to advocate for you. The right LGBTQ family law attorney brings three things a general practitioner usually cannot.

Knowledge of the legal gaps that affect LGBTQ families. The doctrine of "presumed parenthood" — the legal assumption that a person's spouse is also their child's parent — is unevenly applied to same-sex couples and to non-biological parents. After Obergefell v. Hodges in 2015, marriage became national, but the legal infrastructure around parentage, ART, and adoption did not magically update everywhere. An LGBTQ family law attorney knows how to use tools like second-parent adoption, confirmatory adoption, pre-birth parentage orders, Voluntary Declarations of Parentage, and de facto parent petitions to fill those gaps before a crisis hits.

Awareness of state-by-state and post-Dobbs realities. A right that is settled in California may not be settled in Florida, Texas, or Tennessee. A surrogacy contract that holds up in one state may be unenforceable in another. After Dobbs, several states have moved in ways that affect reproductive rights and, by extension, ART and surrogacy for LGBTQ families. A competent LGBTQ family law attorney knows the current law in your jurisdiction, not just the headlines.

Cultural competence as a baseline, not a bonus. Correct pronouns. Chosen names used over legal names where appropriate. Forms that do not assume "husband and wife." Recognition that families look like single parents, three parents, co-parenting non-romantic partnerships, blended chosen families. Trans-inclusive estate documents that hold up in probate. None of that should require translation work from you when you are already dealing with a divorce or a custody fight.

If you want to vet an attorney yourself, the National LGBTQ+ Bar Association's Family Law Institute directory is a respected starting point, and Lambda Legal's Help Desk can point you to free legal services if cost is a barrier. We exist to make that vetting easier — one phone call and we hand you a professional who has already cleared that bar.

What is an LGBTQ family law attorney?

An LGBTQ family law attorney is a lawyer who handles family law matters — divorce, custody, parentage, adoption, surrogacy, estate planning — and has specific experience representing gay, lesbian, bisexual, and transgender clients. They understand the legal nuances that affect LGBTQ families, such as non-biological parent rights, the timing complications of pre-Obergefell relationships, and trans-inclusive document changes.

Why hire an LGBTQ family law attorney instead of a general family lawyer?

General family lawyers may not be familiar with the specific issues LGBTQ clients face — presumed parenthood gaps for non-biological parents, complications around domestic partnerships and civil unions, surrogacy and ART contracts, three-parent recognition under California Family Code §7612(c), and gender marker changes. An LGBTQ family law attorney brings both the legal expertise and the cultural competence needed to advocate for you effectively.
